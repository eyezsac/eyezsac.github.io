## Research ##
### Projects
#### PhD: Dissecting the Drought Regulome of Spring Bread Wheat
My PhD research, 'Dissecting the Drought Regulome', was conducted at the University of York with Dr. Andrea Harper and Prof. Ian Bancroft. Drought is a bit of an existential threat for our crops - over half of the planet will be exposed to droughts yearly by 2050, and this will hit food security drastically, especially in lower- and middle-income countries - and finding novel ways for our plants to combat this is paramount. 

The aim was to pick apart how complex polyploid wheat (*Triticum aestivum*) responds to drought at the transcriptomic level, using differential expression and WGCNA to identify key genes involved in drought responses and drought tolerance, and then to overlay that with the higher regulatory layer of DNA methylation to understand how that gene network is coordinated. 

I identified candidate hub genes, epigenetic signatures, and proposed novel methylation-controlled and transposon-driven regulatory mechanisms that could be of interest for wheat breeders and the field. I've mined a few publications out of this thesis - take a look below!
